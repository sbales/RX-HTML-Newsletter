February is American Heart Month! Heart disease is a known risk factor for chronic kidney disease (CKD)—and is controllable, even preventable in some cases. Take care of your heart and lower your risk of heart disease by eating healthy, exercising, reducing stress and not smoking. Talk to your care team about ways to keep your heart healthy.

 

Fit and fabulous in his golden years

88-year-old Dan DiSalvo doesn’t let anything slow him down. At 71, Dan had a heart attack and then open-heart surgery. During his time in recovery, he started exercising and fell in love with it! Then, 3 years ago, an autoimmune disorder led Dan to start dialysis. When starting dialysis, Dan decided to commit to his regular exercise routine because it made him feel his best. Having a routine helps him thrive outside of dialysis.

Dan exercises regularly with friends at his local YMCA. His friends, as well as his care team, have been so positive and help Dan stay motivated. Dan’s family and friends say he is an inspiration to everyone around him. People at the YMCA stop and watch Dan lifting weights and doing 24 pull ups at a time!

Committed to his health, Dan says that heart disease and dialysis won’t keep him from doing what he loves!

The heart of the matter
 

Are you or a loved one at risk for CKD? Many factors like age, weight, family history and overall health could put you at greater risk. Knowing the signs and symptoms of CKD can help you with early detection. Early detection is key because it can help you make a plan to stay your healthiest.

Getting tested for CKD is especially important if you have any of the known risk factors. During American Heart Month, remember that two risk factors for CKD are high blood pressure and heart disease. If you’ve already been diagnosed with CKD, you will be tested regularly to monitor your health.

Early detection makes a difference! Know your risk factors and talk to your doctor about your screening options.
